1. PRODUCT SUMMARY (WHAT THIS PROJECT IS)
================================================================================

FlowInvoicer is a web application for professional invoicing aimed at
freelancers, designers, developers, contractors, consultants, and small
businesses. The core promise is speed and simplicity: create invoices quickly,
look professional, automate follow-ups (reminders), and track who owes what.

Tagline used in metadata:
"The Fastest Way for Freelancers to Get Paid"

Elevator pitch (from live landing hero):
- Headline idea: Create invoices in seconds. Get paid faster.
- Subhead: Professional invoicing for freelancers and small businesses.
  No fees, no hassle. (Marketing tone; actual plans may charge for premium.)

Differentiators to emphasize in design:
- Fast workflow (60-second / minimal-friction invoicing)
- Professional PDFs and multiple branded templates
- Automated email reminders with tone options
- Estimates with client approval and one-click conversion to invoices
- Analytics and payment status visibility
- Transparent pricing (free tier + monthly + pay-per-invoice)

================================================================================
2. TARGET AUDIENCE & TONE
================================================================================

Who:
- Solo freelancers and agencies
- Creators and indie operators who invoice irregularly or at volume
- Users who want polish without enterprise complexity

Tone for copy and visuals:
- Confident, direct, modern SaaS—not corporate stiff
- Friendly but professional (creators & entrepreneurs, not only CFOs)
- Trust: security, clarity, no hidden fees (matches pricing section messaging)

Avoid:
- Overly technical jargon in hero
- Cluttered "enterprise dashboard" aesthetics unless balanced with whitespace

================================================================================
3. FEATURES (COMPREHENSIVE — MARKETING + APP CAPABILITIES)
================================================================================

A. Core invoicing
- Create invoices quickly; fast mode and detailed line items (discounts, taxes)
- Multiple professional PDF invoice templates (e.g. Minimal, Modern, Creative);
  customizable branding, colors, company info, logo upload
- Invoice statuses: draft, sent, paid, etc.; activity/history
- Public share links for clients to view invoices
- PDF download and email sending (Resend integration in stack)

B. Clients
- Client records: contact info, reuse on new invoices/estimates
- Payment history visibility (conceptually; UI emphasizes tracking)

C. Payments & money
- Display payment instructions (PayPal, Stripe, bank transfer, Venmo, Cash App,
  Google Pay, Apple Pay—as stated on marketing; implementation is user-provided
  methods + manual mark-as-paid)
- Partial payments and payment records where implemented
- Late fees: percentage or fixed, with grace periods
- Write-off: forgive part or all of balance and close invoice (business logic
  in app—optional mention for "serious billing" credibility)

D. Estimates
- Create and send estimates; expiry dates; draft editing
- Client approve/reject via link from email
- Convert approved estimate to invoice in one action

E. Reminders & automation
- Per-invoice reminder schedules
- Reminder tone types: friendly, polite, firm, urgent (marketing copy)
- Scheduled email reminders (queue/cron-related infra in project)

F. Dashboard & analytics
- Revenue-oriented dashboard: pending, overdue, late fees, trends
- Charts/revenue visualization (Recharts in dependencies)

G. Bulk & efficiency (power users)
- Bulk send (status transitions for multiple invoices)
- Bulk mark as paid
- Bulk payment operations where present in API

H. Account & data
- Authentication (Supabase Auth)
- Settings: business profile, payment details, branding
- Data export (PDF/CSV mentioned in FAQ)
- Tax-related fields (database migrations exist—business/tax registration context)

I. Security & compliance (messaging)
- Encrypted storage/transit, no selling client data to third parties
- GDPR-friendly language in FAQ

6. VISUAL & DESIGN SYSTEM (FOLLOW THESE PRINCIPLES)
================================================================================

Overall aesthetic:
- Clean, light, modern SaaS: white backgrounds, lots of whitespace
- Crisp borders (gray-200), subtle hover elevation on cards
- Purple as the single strong brand accent (hero glow, "paid" highlight,
  popular plan). Supporting purples in gradients: #a855f7, #9333ea, #7c3aed
- Primary actions: black buttons for main global CTAs; purple for the
  highlighted pricing tier
- Secondary actions: white fill, gray border

Typography:
- Root layout loads Inter (Google) as --font-inter for body in places
- globals.css theme references Poppins for --font-sans and Space Grotesk for
  --font-heading (class .font-heading on landing headings). For Stitch: use a
  clean geometric or grotesk sans for headlines and a highly readable sans for
  body—consistent with "fast, professional, product UI"

Spacing & layout:
- max-w-7xl containers, centered content
- Responsive: mobile-first; hero stacks; feature grid 2 cols mobile → 3 desktop

Imagery:
- Prefer real product screenshots (dashboard-screenshot.png desktop,
  dashboard-screenshot-mobile.png mobile) framed with border + shadow
- Soft purple radial blur behind hero device mockup for depth (not neon gamer
  aesthetic—keep it subtle)

Motion (optional):
- Gentle scroll-in for cards; floating mockup hover lift; mobile testimonial
  marquee—motion should feel premium, not distracting

Iconography:
- Simple line icons (Lucide-style) in muted gray on light gray tile
  backgrounds

Accessibility:
- Strong contrast for body text (gray-600/900 on white)
- Buttons large enough for touch on mobile
- Do not rely on purple alone for critical information

7. DESIGN PRINCIPLES (CHECKLIST FOR STITCH OUTPUT)
================================================================================

1. Clarity first: one primary message per section; scannable headlines.
2. Speed & ease: visuals and copy should reinforce "minutes, not hours."
3. Trust: social proof, security mentions, transparent pricing table.
4. Consistent brand accent: purple used with discipline; black for primary CTA.
5. Professional polish: invoice/template previews should look print-ready.
6. Mobile parity: hero, pricing, and CTAs must work at small widths.
7. Hierarchy: Popular plan > Free > Pay per invoice in visual weight.
8. Avoid clutter: prefer grid + whitespace over dense feature lists in hero.
